- Focus: Composition, tenure, and functions of the Payments Regulatory Board under the Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007.
- Question quota hint: 4
- Claim count: 4

#### Evidence Bullets

- The Payments Regulatory Board is constituted under the Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007.
- The RBI Governor serves as the ex officio Chairperson of the Board.
- The Board includes three central government nominated experts with a 4-year tenure.
- The Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007 designates RBI as the central authority overseeing payment infrastructure in India.

#### Section Excerpt

The RBI constituted the Payments Regulatory Board under the Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007. The Board consists of the RBI Governor (ex officio Chairperson), Deputy Governor, one RBI officer, and three central government nominees. Members have a 4-year tenure. The Act regulates payment systems in India, with RBI as the central overseeing authority.

### Specialized Bonds: Transition and Additional Tier 1 (AT1) Bonds

- Pages: 23
- Focus: Characteristics and objectives of Transition Bonds and Additional Tier 1 (AT1) Bonds.
- Question quota hint: 3
- Claim count: 4

#### Evidence Bullets

- Transition Bonds are a sub-category of green debt securities as per SEBI regulations.
- Transition Bonds help hard-to-abate sectors raise capital for progressive decarbonization.
- Additional Tier 1 (AT1) Bonds are perpetual bonds issued by banks without a maturity date.
- AT1 bonds include a call option and are issued to meet capital adequacy requirements.

#### Section Excerpt

Transition Bonds are a sub-category of green debt securities under SEBI, aimed at helping hard-to-abate sectors like steel and cement raise capital for decarbonization. Additional Tier 1 (AT1) Bonds, or perpetual bonds, are issued by banks without a maturity date but include a call option. They are used by banks to meet capital adequacy requirements.

#### Claims

- Transition Bonds are classified as a sub-category of green debt securities under SEBI regulations.
- Transition Bonds are designed to help hard-to-abate sectors, such as steel and cement, raise capital for progressive decarbonization.
- Additional Tier 1 (AT1) Bonds are perpetual bonds issued by banks that do not have a maturity date.
- AT1 Bonds include a call option and are issued by banks specifically to meet capital adequacy requirements.
