#### Cloudbursts and Orographic Lifting

- Pages: 97
- Focus: Definition, meteorological mechanisms (orographic lift), and disaster risk reduction strategies for cloudbursts.
- Claims: 4
- Evidence anchors:
  - The IMD defines a cloudburst as an event where 10 cm of rainfall is received at a station in one hour.
  - Cloudbursts are more common in mountainous zones due to orographic lift, where moist air is forced to rise, cooling and condensing rapidly.
  - A violent upward flow of air prevents condensing raindrops from falling until the lack of upward air causes the whole stored water to fall at once.
  - Climate change exacerbates cloudbursts, as every 1°C rise in temperature allows the air to hold approximately 7% more moisture.
